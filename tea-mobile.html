Tea Mobile: Boba Delivery Website

Type

Course project, mobile website

Role

UI Design

Duration

Jun - Jul 2020 (3 weeks)

Tools

Sketch, Photoshop, Balsamiq

A fun and streamlined interface for a boba delivery service

Tea Mobile is a fictional boba shop that delivers to college students in Atlanta. I designed a mobile delivery website for them that is both visually appealing and streamlines the ordering process for customers.

Store concept

  • Store name: Tea Mobile
  • Cost: $ - Affordable
  • Location: Midtown, Atlanta, GA (Urban)
  • Target audience: College students and event organizers for college events
  • Menu: Boba tea, smoothies, coffee (customizable by size, sugar and ice levels, toppings quantity)

Establishing the target audience

To get a better grasp of the goals and tendencies of the target audience I was working with, I listed out different target groups, as well as their demographic and psychographic information. Most of this information came from my personal experience as a student at Georgia Tech and being around avid boba drinkers, as well as looking at boba store websites like T4, MeetFresh, and Kung Fu Tea. However, if I were to repeat this project, I would spend some more time on user research.

Target groups

  • College students who buy boba frequently and want a quick and efficient ordering process that they can go through repeatedly
  • College students who have had boba only a few times and need more help with menu options, customization, and payment
  • Event organizers for small college events, who need to order 10-30 drinks at once

Demographics

  • Gender: all genders
  • Age: 18-30
  • Education: college education
  • Occupations: students, school administration
  • Marital Status: single
  • Income: limited income
  • Location: Georgia Tech (urban, college campus)

Psychographics

  • Personality & Attitudes: social, outspoken, fun, lazy
  • Values: open-minded, work-hard-play-hard attitude
  • Lifestyles: busy and stressed, studious, studies with friends, works at a student job

User personas

User needs, client needs, and sitemap

After learning more about my target users, I listed some main user needs: viewing the menu, customizing orders, viewing their carts, and making payments. In particular, the customization process needed to be efficient for regular customers and easy to learn for new customers, and people ordering in bulk should be able to edit and change the quantities of drinks in their carts.

I also noted the needs of the client, the boba shop. The website needed to communicate the shop's reliability, quality, and vibrant brand identity and also encourage customers to try new/popular items. I decided to address these needs in the website's home screen or through the visual design.

Keeping user and client needs in mind, I outlined the general structure of the website in a sitemap.



Wireframes

I drafted wireframes for the screens listed in the sitemap. I took a lot of inspiration from delivery apps like Starbucks and McDonalds in planning how to organize each screen.



Visual design

I wanted a vibrant look for the website that incorporated boba into the visual theme. I created a moodboard with imagery and UI inspiration to define the general style that I was looking for, and also designed a logo and set visual guidelines for the design.

Future improvements

Although I was quite happy with what I was able to create in a short amount of time, I think there are a few things I could improve on for this project. Notably, I think I could have spent more time on user research by interviewing students and other users to get better insights for my product. I also feel that I could have incorporated more features to cater towards my main audience group, frequent boba buyers, such a login/registration system, reordering past orders, and saving payment info.

Another thing I want to consider in the future is making my design more friendly for developers, as a lot of the graphic elements I used may be time-consuming to implement. I also want to try designing from the shop owner's perspective and consider how they would track orders or change the menu.
