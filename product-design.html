PRODUCT/INTERIOR DESIGN

Jingles

9.5” × 4” × 5.5” (large prototype), foam and cardstock (large prototype), clay (small prototype), color pencil, Photoshop, Autodesk Sketchbook (illustrations)

I wanted to create a playful shoe for young children by combining the form of a bowling shoe and the jingles of a tambourine. I first sculpted a prototype out of clay, then developed my idea by sketching orthographic sketches of the shoe and sculpting a life-sized prototype from foam.

Peingü

8.5" × 14", Photoshop

This is a poster for an ergonomic pitcher and creamer that I designed in a team (Isaac Lerner, Asli Kocaoglu, and Louis Dubarry). The pitcher is built to redistribute the weight of water to make it easier to pour. I illustrated the pitcher and creamer and designed the poster.

Bamboo

19” × 19” × 42” (chair), cardboard and bicycle tube (chair), Photoshop and Autodesk Sketchbook (illustrations)

Inspired by a bamboo grove, I designed this chair with a rounded seat and a back panel made of cardboard strips of different heights. It can seat an adult and be disassembled and reassembled for shipping.

2010, Part 2

1762 px × 992 px, Maya

This is an interior model of a treehouse, similar to those I pictured in my head as a 9-year-old. It has a minimal, cozy, and bright vibe that would have been perfect for my childhood playdates. Part 1 can be found in the Art page.
