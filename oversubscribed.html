Oversubscribed Design Process


Ideation

Tagline: Track Your Trials, See Your Subscriptions

Description: Oversubscribed is an app that helps everyday users keep track of their free trials and subscriptions (ex. Amazon Prime, Netflix, Photoshop). Many people sign up for free trials, miss the cancellation date, and end up paying for the service, or they subscribe to a monthly service and keep paying for it even when they’ve stopped using it. This app solves this problem by organizing a list of subscriptions and notifying users when they are about to pay for a subscription.

Functions:

  • Organizes a list of free trials and a list of subscriptions
  • Provides a graph breaking down how much money a user spent on subscriptions that month
  • Allows users to set a budget
  • Allows users to manage notifications for payment/expiration alerts

Audience: Young adults who are learning to manage their finances; they use free trials and limit subscriptions to save money

  • People who are learning to manage their finances and save money
  • People who have some disposable income they can use on things like Netflix and Amazon Prime and magazines
  • People who are forgetful
  • People with large families that they share services with (ex. Netflix, Spotify Premium Family)

User Profile:

Alex from Los Angeles:

  • Alex is an 18-year-old who has just started college.
  • He has a part-time job that doesn’t pay much.
  • He has a small passion for graphic design.
  • He created 10 emails for 10 free trials of Adobe Photoshop.
  • He loves listening to music on Spotify and watching movies on Netflix.
  • He and his 3 friends split the cost of a homework-help subscription service.
  • He has a habit of overspending.

Goals:

  • Political​: Discourage how companies take advantage of their users’ forgetfulness to earn money
  • Financial:​ ​Gain more users and earn money through donations or ad revenue
  • Social:​ Teach young adults to manage their money
  • Functional:​ Provide a seamless and minimal experience for users

Similar Apps:

  • TrackMySubs: ​Has similar functionalities but more complicated and designed for businesses.
  • Subscriptions:​ ​Also has similar functionalities but it doesn’t emphasize/notify about upcoming payment dates.
  • Money Manager: ​ General money-management app that breaks down how a user allocates their money every month.
