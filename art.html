ART

Ruin

11” × 8”, pen

I wanted to portray the devastating effects that pollution, especially plastic pollution, has on the natural environment. I used stippling to create a dense smoke of plastic goods like bottles, food wrappers, and trash bags that fly out of a city and envelop an octopus.

Away

24” × 18”, acrylic on masonite board

Inspired by David Salle’s collage-like paintings, this painting reflects the chaotic feelings of homesickness and confinement through the journey of a fish from the sea to the fishbowl. I used contrasting colors and sharp lines to emphasize the turbulence of being away from home and the frantic attempts at escape.

Beryl Flare

4800 px × 2700 px, Photoshop

This concept design for a cave was inspired by canine teeth, with large molar monoliths framing a path towards a bright blue glow. I worked with a team (Jaiminy Qiu, Kevin Yang) who designed a character and a vehicle to add to the landscape. The final work with the character and vehicle can be found here: link

Bikini Bottom, 3019 A.D.

1800 px × 3600 px, Photoshop

The underwater world is typically known for its beauty, with colorful schools of fish and rolling blue waves. This digital painting of cannibalistic fish shows an apocalyptic take on a once-thriving ecosystem. I used a complementary color scheme and muted colors for this work.

Final December

1800 px × 1725 px, Photoshop

This is a character design for a widow living alone in a cave, longing for the days of youth. Her design is inspired by long-nosed villains such as the witch in Snow White. The title of this work, Final December, reflects the character’s frailness in her last days.

Fine Line

6991 px × 3300 px, Photoshop

This sewing machine, inspired by sewing machines from the 1800s, was designed as a prop for the character in Final December. It has a vintage and worn-down look to match the character.

2010, Part 1

3300 px × 4200 px (original image), Photoshop and Maya

I recreated a dreamy treehouse forest I imagined in my 9-year-old head through the use of atmospheric perspective, calm colors, and dappled lighting. I started out with thumbnails, then a 3D representation on Maya, then a value painting, and finally, the finished, colored piece. Part 2 can be found in the Product/Interior Design page.

Laundry Day

20” × 16”, oil on canvas

I painted a golden retriever from the perspective of the inside of a washing machine, with the dark walls of the washing machine creating a vignette effect on the bright subject. This painting taught me to look creatively at everyday appliances and allowed me to explore unique perspectives.

Mandrill

16” × 20”, oil on canvas

This is a painting of a mandrill from Jungle Park, Tenerife, Spain, based off of a photo.

Image reference: link

Self Portrait

16” × 20”, oil on canvas

This is a painting of myself surrounded by ingredients from Chinese and American cuisines, exploring a blend of the two cultures I’ve grown up around.

Lakeview

20” × 16”, oil on canvas

This is a painting of Moraine Lake, AB, Canada, based off of a photo.

Image reference: link

Adore Me Too

13" × 16", color pencil

This drawing portrays a seagull trying to eat bird feed out of a birdhouse made for sparrows. This humorously criticizes how humans adore small creatures for their cuteness while detesting larger creatures who do virtually the same thing as their smaller counterparts.

Nature's Glitch

16” × 20”, acrylic on campus

This painting shows a seagull photobombing the Windows XP desktop background, spoiling the pleasant background with its bitter expression.

Alter Seago

12" × 9", pen, watercolor, Photoshop

This drawing depicts how animals are often babyfied to make cute toys. The plump, beady-eyed seagull toy on the left contrasts with a ruffled, unamused seagull on the right.

In N Out

9" × 12", pen

This illustration depicts how neighborhood seagulls used to invade and steal children's lunches at my school.Their desperate faces and wide mouths reveal their gluttonous nature.

Ocean's Ways

25.5" × 33", Photoshop

Inspired by the traditional art of Native Americans living in the Pacific Northwest, I illustrated interactions among various marine animals using the colors and style of the traditional art. I started with the illustration of the squid and the turtle, duplicated it, then added in the crab and small fish to create a seamless pattern.

The Day After Yesterday

18” × 7.5”, charcoal

I drew a skull from multiple perspectives, using simple line art for the first and last images, line art combined with shading for the second and fourth images, and realism for the middle image.
